<p><p>The nucleic acid electrophoresis reagent market consists of two primary types: gel electrophoresis and capillary electrophoresis. Gel electrophoresis involves using a gel medium, such as agarose or polyacrylamide, to separate nucleic acids based on size, allowing for visualization and analysis. Capillary electrophoresis, on the other hand, utilizes thin capillaries filled with a polymer solution, offering high resolution and faster analysis times. Both techniques are essential for genetic research, diagnostics, and various molecular biology applications.</p></p>
