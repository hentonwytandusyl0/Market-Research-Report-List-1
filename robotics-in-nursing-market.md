<p><p>Robotics in the nursing market includes two primary types: preoperative auxiliary care and postoperative auxiliary care. Preoperative auxiliary care focuses on robotic systems that assist in patient preparation and education, enhancing efficiency and accuracy in procedures such as assessments and scheduling. Conversely, postoperative auxiliary care involves robotic technologies that aid in recovery, offering monitoring, medication delivery, and physical assistance, which enhances patient comfort and supports nurses in managing postoperative care more effectively, ultimately improving patient outcomes and satisfaction.</p></p>
